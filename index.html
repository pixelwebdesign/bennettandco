Practice areas

What we handle

We practise in three areas of law. Each is led by a solicitor with specific experience and qualifications in that field. We do not take on matters outside these areas, and we will always tell you if another firm would be better placed to assist.

I.

Commercial Law

We advise on business transactions and commercial matters for owner-operated businesses and medium-sized companies throughout the Bay of Plenty. Our work in this area covers the full lifecycle of a business — from initial structuring and shareholder arrangements through to the sale or acquisition of an enterprise. James Bennett has practised commercial law for over 25 years and has advised on a wide range of transactions across multiple industries.

Business sales & acquisitions Shareholder agreements Joint ventures Commercial contracts Company formation & structure Corporate governance
II.

Property Law

Our property practice covers residential and commercial transactions, subdivision, leasing, and development work across the Bay of Plenty. Sarah Rowe leads this area and holds NZLS accredited specialist status in Property Law — a designation reflecting her depth of experience and focus in this field. We act for buyers and sellers, landlords and tenants, developers, and financiers.

Residential conveyancing Commercial transactions Subdivision & development Leases & licences Body corporate matters Easements & covenants
III.

Employment Law

We act for both employers and employees across the full range of employment matters under New Zealand law. Daniel Parata leads this practice area and has experience appearing before the Employment Relations Authority, conducting mediations, and advising on employment matters at all stages — from drafting employment agreements through to resolving disputes. His background on both sides of the employer-employee relationship gives him a clear-eyed view of how matters are likely to proceed.

Employment agreements Personal grievances Restructuring & redundancy ERA proceedings Workplace investigations Health & safety advice

The firm

About Bennett & Co

Bennett & Co was established in Tauranga in 1998 by James Bennett, following fifteen years in commercial legal practice in Auckland. The firm was set up to offer businesses and individuals in the Bay of Plenty direct access to experienced legal advice — without the overhead and impersonal service that can sometimes characterise larger practices.

Over more than two decades, the firm has grown steadily through word of mouth and client referrals. We have three solicitors, each with a defined area of expertise, and we keep our practice deliberately focused. Clients deal directly with the solicitor handling their file — not with intermediaries or rotating staff — which means continuity, accountability, and fewer things falling through the gaps.

We do not take on every type of legal work. Where a matter is outside our experience or better suited to a specialist firm, we say so plainly and point clients in the right direction.

How we work

What to expect

A few things worth knowing about how we handle instructions, communicate with clients, and approach fees — before you decide whether to get in touch.

01

You deal directly with your solicitor

When you instruct us, you deal directly with the solicitor handling your matter — not a legal executive, not a different person each time you call. That means better continuity, more accountability, and clearer communication throughout.

02

Fee estimates before we begin

We provide a fee estimate or, where the scope allows, a fixed fee before commencing work. If something changes and is likely to affect costs, we will let you know in advance — not at the end of the matter when it is too late to make a different decision.

03

Plain language, not legal jargon

We explain your situation and options in plain language. Legal matters can involve complexity, but our role is to make your position understandable and help you make informed decisions — not to make the process feel more complicated than it needs to be.

04

Honest about the limits of our practice

We do not accept instructions in areas outside our practice. If your matter is better suited to a specialist firm — or requires expertise we do not have — we will say so directly and, where we can, suggest an appropriate referral.
